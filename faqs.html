üì° Starlink & Internet

I live in the woods (East Texas Pine Curtain). Will Starlink work?
Maybe, but you probably need elevation. Starlink requires a roughly 100-degree clear view of the northern sky. If you have 80ft pine trees, a standard roof mount won't cut it. We specialize in custom tree-topping mounts and tower mounts to clear the canopy. I do a full obstruction test before drilling a single hole.
Can you get the wire inside without drilling a hole in my wall?
Usually, yes. We prefer to route cables through soffit vents, attic spaces, or existing cable entry points to keep your siding sealed. If we absolutely must drill, we use industrial-grade feed-through bushings and silicone sealant to ensure it is 100% waterproof and pest-proof.
I'm a gamer. Will Starlink lag?
I play Xbox myself (Halo fan here), so I get it. Latency on Starlink is generally 25ms-50ms, which is decent for gaming. However, obstructions cause lag spikes. If your dish sees a tree branch every 45 seconds, you will lag out. My installation focuses on getting you 0% obstructions for the smoothest gaming experience possible.
Can I take my Starlink dish camping?
Yes, if you have the "Roam" plan or the Mini. However, moving the standard dish is a pain if it's bolted to your roof. For RVers, I recommend either a flat-mount conversion (for serious travelers) or a quick-disconnect pole mount on your ladder so you can grab it and go.

‚ö° Electrical & Safety

My outlet feels warm. Is that normal?
No. Stop using it immediately. A warm dimmer switch is normal; a warm outlet is a fire hazard. It usually means the wire connection is loose and arcing (creating heat). Call me ASAP. Do not ignore it.
Can you install a ceiling fan where there is no light?
Yes, but it requires running new wire and installing a "fan-rated" brace box in the ceiling. A standard plastic light box will not hold a 50lb spinning fan (it will eventually fall). I only install fans on steel, structural-rated boxes for safety.
Do you do full panel upgrades?
No. I focus on technical repairs and downstream electrical (outlets, switches, fixtures, sub-panels for sheds). For a full 200-amp main service panel replacement, you need a Master Electrician to pull a permit with the city. I stay in my lane.

üöê RV & Mobile Tech

My RV breaker keeps tripping when the AC is on.
This is common in East Texas summers. It's often a "Soft Start" issue. Your AC pulls massive amps (LRA) to start up. I can install a Soft Start capacitor that reduces that startup spike by 70%, allowing you to run your AC on a smaller generator or a standard 30-amp hookup without tripping.
Do you fix RV slide-outs?
Yes. Whether it's a 12v motor failure, a sheared pin, or a hydraulic leak (I have hydraulics experience), I can diagnose it. Note: If the frame itself is bent/twisted, that is a body shop job, but 90% of issues are mechanical or electrical.
I live in my RV full-time. Can you help with winterizing?
Absolutely. I lived full-time in an RV myself. I can help you skirt it, heat-tape your water hose, and inspect your furnace before the freeze hits.
